Here's the side-by-side takeaway from this exercise:

Which feels more personalized? Prompt B — without question. It knows you're a student building toward a portfolio, learns by doing, and has serious time to invest. Every week ends with a deployable project, not just a reading assignment.

Which would you actually follow? Prompt B. Prompt A's Python roadmap was guessed — you might not even want Python. Prompt B is a contract you made with yourself by filling in the context.

What role did context play? Context is the difference between a template and a plan. The AI model didn't change — the same model produced both outputs. What changed was the information it had to work with. Five short answers eliminated all ambiguity and let the model make dozens of small decisions (pacing, project type, resources, outcome) that were actually right for you.

This is the core lesson of Context Engineering: the quality of AI output is largely determined by the quality of context you provide, not just the quality of the prompt itself.
