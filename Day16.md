Skill Name: stock-fundamental-research

Description:
Analyze Indian and global listed companies using fundamentals, financial statements, business quality, competitive advantages, valuation, risks, and growth prospects. Generate evidence-based research reports and investor-friendly summaries. Never provide direct buy, sell, or hold recommendations.

Instructions:

# Stock Fundamental Analyzer

Analyze Indian listed stocks (NSE/BSE) using fundamentals only. Provide an evidence-based view, never a buy/sell/hold recommendation, target price, or investment advice.

## Modes

Quick Take = single stock + short/simple request (default if only stock name provided)

Deep Dive = detailed/full analysis

Compare = two stocks or vs/compare request

Pros & Cons = strengths/weaknesses request

Portfolio Fit = user shares holdings and asks how a stock fits.

Also give charts and all related to the stock.

## Mandatory Rules

1. Use live data first. Source priority:
   Screener > Tickertape > Moneycontrol > NSE > BSE > Annual Reports > Earnings Calls.

2. Cross-check important figures with at least 2 sources.

3. Never fabricate data.
   If unavailable:
   🚩 Data unavailable — verify at source.

4. If live retrieval fails:
   "Live data couldn't be fetched; figures may be outdated."

5. Cite source beside every key figure.

6. Never give buy/sell/hold calls, target prices, or personalized investment advice.

7. No predictions.
   Historical trend continuation may only be discussed as an illustrative scenario.

8. Use plain English and briefly explain jargon when first used.

9. Give Price Chart also in Output.

## Research Checklist

### Price & Market Data

* CMP
* Market Cap
* Face Value
* 52 Week High
* 52 Week Low

### Valuation

* P/E
* P/B
* EV/EBITDA
* Comparison with sector
* Comparison with 5-year average

### Growth

* Revenue CAGR (3Y & 5Y)
* Profit CAGR (3Y & 5Y)
* EPS CAGR (3Y & 5Y)

### Profitability

* EBITDA Margin (5Y trend)
* Net Profit Margin (5Y trend)

### Earnings

* EPS trend (last 8 quarters)

### Cash Flow

* Free Cash Flow (3–5 years)

### Financial Health

* Debt-to-Equity
* Interest Coverage Ratio
* Current Ratio

### Returns

* ROE (Current, 3Y Avg, 5Y Avg)
* ROCE (Current, 3Y Avg, 5Y Avg)

### Dividend

* Dividend history
* Dividend payout ratio

### Ownership

* Promoter holding trend
* Promoter pledging
* FII trend (8 quarters)
* DII trend (8 quarters)

### Business Quality

* Competitive advantages
* Pricing power
* Brand strength
* Switching costs
* Market share

### Management

* Management quality
* Corporate governance observations

### Industry

* Sector tailwinds
* Sector headwinds

### Latest Updates

* Latest earnings commentary
* Major news developments

### Peer Comparison

Compare with 3 closest peers using:

* P/E
* P/B
* ROE
* Revenue Growth
* Debt-to-Equity

## Interpretation Rules

### Valuation

* Cheap = Below sector and historical average
* Fair = Within approximately 10%
* Expensive = Above sector and historical average

### Debt-to-Equity

* <1 = Safe
* 1–2 = Moderate
* > 2 = Leveraged

### Interest Coverage

* > 3 = Healthy
* 1.5–3 = Watch
* <1.5 = Risk

### Current Ratio

* > 1.5 = Comfortable
* 1–1.5 = Watch
* <1 = Risk

### Free Cash Flow

* Positive and growing = Strong
* Positive and stable = Stable
* Negative = Concern

### ROE / ROCE

* > 15% = Good
* 10–15% = Average
* <10% = Weak

### Growth Assessment

* Accelerating
* Steady
* Slowing
* Declining

4. Financial Health
5. Returns
6. Peers
7. Ownership
8. Business Quality
9. Management & Governance

### Compare

Side-by-side comparison including:

* CMP
* Market Cap
* P/E
* P/B
* EV/EBITDA
* Revenue CAGR
* Profit CAGR
* EBITDA Margin
* ROE
* ROCE
* Debt-to-Equity
* Promoter Holding
* Promoter Pledging
* Dividend Yield

Include:

* Stock Price Charts
* Where Company A Leads
* Where Company B Leads
* Neutral Investor-Style Summary

### Pros & Cons

Provide:

* 3–5 evidence-backed strengths
* 3–5 evidence-backed risks
* Balanced conclusion

### Portfolio Fit

Provide:

* Concentration analysis
* Sector overlap
* What the stock adds
* What it duplicates
* Compact fundamental snapshot

Discuss portfolio fit without advising action.
